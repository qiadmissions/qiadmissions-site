The Method

How Quintessence works.

An account of the philosophy behind the firm, the way we work with a student from ninth grade through senior year, the essay process itself, and what makes the work excellent.

The name

Quintessence is the fifth essence — in the old cosmology, the pure element beyond earth, water, air, and fire, what remains when everything inessential is stripped away. The firm is named for that idea rather than for the service, because the work begins there: helping a student discover, cultivate, and communicate the deepest essence of who they are. The application is where that becomes visible to a college. It is the expression of the philosophy, not the philosophy itself.

We treat an application as an art rather than a machine — a composition built from the real material of one student's life, not a form filled against a template.

The Five Elements

The method moves through five phases, in order, from the most open to the most finished. They give the name its second meaning — quint-essence, the five elements — and the same five shape a single essay, the whole application, and the years that lead up to it.

Space · Framing

Before a word is written, we build the whole picture — the full context: the student, and the colleges they are aiming at. The richer this groundwork, the truer everything that follows.

  • Clarify the goal, and what these colleges are really looking for.
  • Gather and organize everything that makes the student who they are.
  • Define the landscape, and begin building the right list of schools.
Fire · Focusing

The most important thinking in the process. Everything we have gathered becomes one deliberate strategy, and everything after this point follows from it.

  • Find the seed idea: the core of who this student is.
  • Map what goes where, so each essay carries its own part of the story.
  • Build an Essay Blueprint for every piece.
Water · Flowing

Now the student writes. With the Essay Blueprint in hand, the work is genuinely theirs, and this is where the truest, most surprising material tends to surface.

  • The student uses the Essay Blueprint to write a draft, in their own voice.
  • Slow by design: the depth shows up here, not in a rush.
Air · Fixing

Revision at the level of substance and strategy, not commas. We make each piece stronger, and shape the separate essays into one coherent application.

  • Strengthen the structure and the strategy.
  • Make sure each piece does its job and the whole holds together.
Earth · Finalizing

The last stretch, and a real one: the distance between a strong draft and something genuinely ready to send.

  • Line-level polish, with every requirement met.
  • Finalizing, not finishing: a strong draft is not yet a finished one.

From ninth grade to senior year

We work with families across the whole arc, not only in the final year. For younger students the work is counseling — building toward a strong application over several years: the activities and direction, the developing sense of who the student is becoming, and in time the right list of schools. For seniors it is the application itself, the full method run from first strategy to final submission across the application season.

Either way it is one advisory built around a single student — a complete relationship, not a service brought in for a single task.

The essay process

The essays are where most of the visible work happens. Each one starts with a plan: what the essay is for, which part of the student's story it carries, and how it fits alongside the rest. From there the student writes, and we guide the structure and the strategy, draft after draft, until each essay does its job and sounds unmistakably like them.

The student is the author throughout. We guide; they write — and that is as much a matter of quality as of principle, because the strongest application is the one that is genuinely theirs.

Our specialty

What we do best, above everything, is narrative — finding the true story in a life and building an application around it. That is a writer's craft, and it is the center of the firm. We hold every application to one standard: that it be the best version of itself, particular to the single student it belongs to. Excellence, not pedigree, is the difference.

Adam Obedian, who founded Quintessence, studied Philosophy and Drama at Columbia. Most families begin with Art of the Essay, the course where a student first learns the method.

What it comes to

A student who finishes the process understanding themselves more clearly than when they began — and an application that is, in every line, genuinely their own.
